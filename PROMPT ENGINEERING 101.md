The scope question first:
Prompt engineering as a tutorial series has a positioning problem — everyone has heard of it, most content about it is either too shallow (just "be specific and clear") or too academic (papers nobody applies). The gap is a series that treats it as a craft with mechanics, not a collection of tips. The right framing is: prompting is the primary interface between you and the most powerful tools ever built — learning it properly is a leverage multiplier on everything else you do.
The series also has a natural progression: you start with understanding how the model actually reads your prompt, then you learn the fundamental techniques, then you apply them to increasingly complex scenarios, then you apply them specifically to agents. That last part is the bridge to the agent series.
Clean split into four volumes:

Volume 1 — How Models Actually Read Your Prompts (mental models, not tips)
Volume 2 — Core Techniques (the fundamental toolkit)
Volume 3 — Advanced Patterns (chaining, evaluation, system design)
Volume 4 — Prompt Engineering for AI Agents (the bridge to the agent series)
